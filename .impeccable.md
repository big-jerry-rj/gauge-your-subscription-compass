# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Productivity-focused individuals managing 5–10 subscriptions and spending $90+/month without visibility. They use the app during brief check-ins (commute, morning routine) — glance-and-go behaviour is the core use case. The job to be done: instant financial clarity and zero loose ends.

### Brand Personality
**Confident & Alive.** Three words: *sharp*, *reactive*, *trusted*.

Data is the hero — zero decoration or filler. The interface has pulse: bold numbers, a single punchy accent (electric lime `hsl(74 100% 50%)`), and enough motion to feel reactive without being noisy. Think: a Bloomberg terminal that grew up on iOS and knows how to dress. The dark background and clean Plus Jakarta Sans typography are the confidence; the lime is the personality.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-first. Light mode is clean fallback, never the primary experience.
- **Primary color**: Electric lime `hsl(74 100% 50%)` — used sparingly as a single accent, never for decoration.
- **Typography**: Plus Jakarta Sans, weights 600–900 for data, 400–500 for labels. Numbers are always the largest element in any data view.
- **Radius**: iOS-native squircle language — `rounded-2xl` (24px) for cards, `rounded-xl` (20px) for inputs, `rounded-full` for chips and badges.
- **Motion**: Framer Motion spring physics (`stiffness: 300, damping: 30`) for positional changes; `duration: 0.18–0.25s ease-out` for fades. No bouncy or decorative animation — every motion communicates state.
- **Anti-references**: No glassmorphism, no gradient fills on cards, no emoji in UI chrome, no rounded-pill buttons on primary actions, no heavy drop shadows.

### Design Principles
1. **Numbers first.** Every screen leads with the most important number. Labels and context are secondary — smaller, muted.
2. **Density with breathing room.** Pack data tightly but never feel cramped. Use consistent 4px-base spacing and let generous vertical rhythm do the work.
3. **One accent, used deliberately.** Lime appears only on: primary CTAs, active states, positive trend indicators, and the Gauge Score ring when healthy. Never as background fill.
4. **Motion communicates, never decorates.** Transitions should reinforce spatial relationships (page slides for lateral navigation, vertical reveals for expansion). Spring physics for drag, ease-out for entrance.
5. **Progressive disclosure.** Show the critical summary above the fold. Let the user pull down more detail. Never overwhelm on first glance.
